Malindig which holds a special place in local folklore. One popular legend tells of a beautiful princess named Maring who enjoyed hunting on the mountain. During one of her hunts, she met a nobleman named Duque. They fell in love, but Maring's father, King Alab, opposed their union. To determine Maring's suitor, the king organized a ship race among her admirers. Duque, lacking resources, couldn't participate. Maring prayed to the gods for intervention, and a storm sank the ships of the competing suitors. The king, seeing this as a sign, allowed Maring to marry Duque. Why Visit Marinduque?

Marinduque offers a peaceful escape with its unspoiled beaches, cultural festivals like Moriones, and lush green mountains. It is ideal for nature lovers, history buffs, and those seeking serenity.

Best Time to Travel

The dry season from December to May is the best time to visit Marinduque. You can enjoy the full beauty of the beaches, join local festivals, and hike without rainy interruptions.

The island's coastal charm is best experienced through its remote and stunning beaches. With warm turquoise waters, swaying palm trees, and golden sunsets, Marinduque is a hidden gem for beach lovers. Enjoy swimming, snorkeling, or simply relaxing with a fresh coconut in hand while locals share their warm hospitality.
